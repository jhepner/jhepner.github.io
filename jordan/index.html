We Take a Different Approach

Our number one priority is community

The District of Columbia’s Tenant Opportunity to Purchase Act (TOPA) has long pitted tenants against landlords and Realtors. The process is often misunderstood on all sides and tenants are frequently misled or uninformed of their rights.

New Horizon Communities takes a different approach to TOPA by embracing the process. Since we do not represent the buyer or seller in these transactions, we work directly with tenants. We offer free tenant consultations and can help tenants initiate the TOPA process, with no commitment or pressure.

New Horizon Communities engages tenants with care, consideration and compassion. Each tenant’s situation is different and we listen to these needs. Depending on the property, we are able to provide packages that can include, but are not limited to, cash payments, free and/or stabilized rent, property upgrades, relocation assistance, condo conversion discount and more.

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers

Why didn’t my landlord or their real estate agent tell me that I had rights?

Landlords and their real estate agents do not represent your interests and are often looking to complete the sale of your home as quickly as possible. When tenants assign their TOPA rights, the sales process is temporarily stalled and the landlord must temporarily wait to sell his property.

Why should I register an interest in purchasing my home if I can’t afford it?

Registering a ‘Statement of Interest’ with the landlord and the Department of Housing and Community Development allows tenants to explore their options and remain in their home for an additional 2-4 months. This does not require showing any financial information or making a deposit, and you are NOT obligated to purchase your home. If you don’t assign your rights by the end of the time period, your landlord can proceed with selling the property to the current buyer. New Horizon Communities can help you register the necessary paperwork in order to help you explore your options.

I already signed something that says I won’t purchase my home. Do I still have rights?

Please contact New Horizon Communities to evaluate any documents you have signed. Our legal team can determine if you still have rights or if you are able to challenge anything you have signed.

How much are my tenant rights worth? Do I have to move when I assign my rights to purchase my home?

Every property that is subject to TOPA is different and the sales price offered to tenants determines what can be offered. In some cases tenants do not need to leave their homes, while in other situations vacating the property would be required. New Horizon Communities listens to tenant needs and in many instances can provide several options to consider. Cash payouts generally start at $2000 and go up.

If I want to take a cash payment for my rights, how quickly will I get paid?

Cash payouts are structured in multiple payments. A typical payout scenario often consists of an initial payment upon signing an agreement, a secondary payment when a contract is ratified by the owner of your property, a third when you provide us a lease and a final payment within 7 days of the rental being made vacant. These payout plans can be catered to different tenant situations.
